About Me.





I'm currently studying for a PhD at the UKRI Centre for Doctoral Training in Artificial Intelligence at Queen Mary University of London as part of their fifth cohort of researchers (2023-2027). My project is focusing on the ways in which deep learning techniques can be improved and optimised with the aim of making deep music generation a more sustainable practice, supervised by Dr Ahmed Sayed.

Prior to my PhD I studied for an MSc in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence, also at Queen Mary University of London, where my thesis was centred on the application of deep learning to the mixdown process of music production, specifically for the mixing of drums. I've been producing music and DJing for over a decade, and I enjoy spending much of free time at gigs and festivals, so music has always been a big part of my life outside of my studies and being able to marry work and play is a real treat.

Aside from AI and music, another big interest of mine is space and the cosmos, as before pursuing a career in computer science I studied for an MSci in Astrophysics, also at Queen Mary University of London. The dark side of the universe always fascinated me, and as such both of my projects, third and fourth years, focused on dark matter and dark energy respectively.
